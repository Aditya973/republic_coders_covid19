Vaccination in India

Two of the Covid–19 vaccines available in India are Covaxin by Bharat Biotech and Covishield by the Serum Institute of India, both been authorized for emergency use in India.
  • Covaxin is Inactivated vaccine. Inactivated means that the virus used for the vaccine has no chance of infecting or replicating the receiver inside the body after the Jab has been given. Bharat biotech had given a commitment that it has a stockpile of 20 million doses of Covaxin and is also aiming for 700 million doses by the end of the year.
    • Dosage - Bharat Biotech stated that the second shot would be given after 14 days to 28 days.
    • Efficacy - 81% according to interim 3rd phase trial results.
    • Side Effects - Headeach, muscle pain, fever, swelling at the injection site. It can last upto 24 hours or more in case if it even lasts after 3 days consult a doctor.
    • Workability against the delta variant - Efficacy rate of Covaxin against the delta variant is dropped to 61%.
    • Pricing -Covaxin is available free of cost at government hospitals. However the price of each dose is ₹1410 at private hospitals.
  • Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine is produced locally by the Serum Institute of India, the world’s largest manufacturer of vaccines. It is said to produce more than 50 million doses a month. The vaccine, known as Covishield, is made from a weakened version of the common cold virus (known as adenovirus) from chimpanzees. It has been modified to look more like coronavirus, though it causes illness.
    • Dosage - The gap between doses of Covishield is at least 84 to 104 days.
    • Efficacy - 90% according to interim 3rd phase trial results.
    • Side Effects - Inflamation, fattigue, fever, burning eyes and swelling at the injection site. Any individual with severe allergies should check the constituents initially before taking the jab
    • Workability against the delta variant - Efficacy rate of Covishield against the delta variant is dropped to 65%.
    • Pricing -Covishield is available free of cost at government hospitals. However the price of each dose is ₹780 at private hospitals.
